# Vendor Performance Analysis Project

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing vendor performance, sales trends, and inventory efficiency in the retail and wholesale industry. The main objective of this analysis is to understand how different vendors and brands contribute to overall business performance and profitability.

The project identifies top-performing vendors, underperforming brands, and evaluates how purchasing strategies affect profit margins and inventory turnover. By combining SQL, Python, and Power BI, this analysis provides actionable insights that can help businesses improve decision-making and optimize their supply chain.

---

## Business Problem

Effective inventory and sales management are critical for maximizing profitability in retail and wholesale businesses. Companies must ensure that they are not losing revenue due to inefficient pricing strategies, slow inventory turnover, or over-dependency on a limited number of vendors.

The goals of this analysis are:

* Identify underperforming brands that may require promotional or pricing adjustments
* Determine the vendors contributing the most to total sales and profit
* Analyze how bulk purchasing impacts unit cost and profitability
* Evaluate inventory turnover to reduce holding costs
* Compare profit margins between high-performing and low-performing vendors

## Key Business Questions

1. Which vendors contribute the most to total sales?
2. Which brands are underperforming?
3. What is the impact of bulk purchasing on unit costs?
4. Which vendors have slow inventory turnover?
5. How does profit margin vary between high-performing and low-performing vendors?

---

## Key Insights

* Total Sales: **$441.41M**
* Total Purchases: **$307.34M**
* Gross Profit: **$134.07M**
* Profit Margin: **38.7%**
* Unsold Inventory Capital: **$2.71M**

These metrics provide an overview of the company’s financial performance and operational efficiency.

---

## Answers to Business Questions

### 1. Which vendors contribute the most to total sales?

The analysis shows that **DIAGEO North America Inc** is the top-performing vendor with approximately **$68M in sales**, followed by **Martignetti Company** with about **$39M**, and **Pernod Ricard USA** with around **$32M** in sales.

Overall, the **top 10 vendors contribute approximately 65.7% of total purchases**, showing that a significant portion of revenue is concentrated among a small group of suppliers.

---

### 2. Which brands are underperforming?

The top-performing brands include:

* **Jack Daniel’s** – $8.0M
* **Tito’s Handmade Vodka** – $7.4M
* **Grey Goose** – $7.2M

Brands such as **Kahlúa** and **Tanqueray** show relatively lower sales compared to the leading brands, suggesting that they may require improved marketing strategies or pricing optimization.

---

### 3. What is the impact of bulk purchasing on unit costs?

The analysis indicates that **bulk purchasing significantly reduces the unit cost of products**. Vendors supplying large quantities often provide volume discounts, which helps reduce procurement costs.

Lower unit costs improve profit margins and allow businesses to maintain competitive pricing strategies in the market.

---

### 4. Which vendors have slow inventory turnover?

Several vendors show relatively lower performance and slower inventory turnover, including:

* Dunn Wine Brokers
* Circa Wines
* Park Street Imports
* Highland Wine
* Alisa Carr Beverages

Slow-moving inventory from these vendors results in capital being tied up in unsold stock, which reduces operational efficiency.

---

### 5. How does profit margin vary between high-performing and low-performing vendors?

The analysis shows that profit margins differ significantly across vendors.

High-performing vendors typically generate higher sales with stable profit margins, while some vendors show high margins but lower sales volumes. Low-performing vendors often experience both lower sales and inconsistent profit margins.

This highlights the importance of balancing sales volume with profit margin when evaluating vendor performance.

---

## Major Findings

* The **top 10 vendors contribute 65.7% of total purchases**, indicating high vendor concentration.
* Bulk purchasing strategies significantly reduce unit costs.
* Some brands maintain high profit margins but lower sales volumes.
* Approximately **$2.71M in capital is tied up in unsold inventory**, indicating potential inventory inefficiencies.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations can help improve business performance:

* Optimize pricing strategies for low-performing brands
* Diversify vendor partnerships to reduce dependency risks
* Improve inventory turnover through better inventory management
* Increase marketing efforts for high-margin but low-sales brands

---
## Conclusion

This project provided a comprehensive analysis of vendor performance, sales contribution, and inventory efficiency. The analysis revealed that a small group of vendors contributes a significant portion of total sales, indicating a high dependency on a limited number of suppliers.

The study also highlighted that bulk purchasing plays an important role in reducing unit costs, which positively impacts profit margins. However, some vendors and brands show slower sales and inventory turnover, leading to unsold inventory capital and reduced operational efficiency.

By identifying top-performing vendors, underperforming brands, and inventory inefficiencies, the analysis provides valuable insights that can help businesses optimize procurement strategies, improve inventory management, and enhance overall profitability.

Overall, this project demonstrates how data analytics tools such as SQL, Python, and Power BI can be used to transform raw data into actionable business insights that support better decision-making.
